William Shakespeare Competition

How much do you remember about his life, times, and works?

Game Rules

Overview: In three rounds, your team will recall facts about William Shakespeare from memory and your notes.

  • Round 1: The Times of Shakespeare
  • Round 2: The Life of Shakespeare
  • Round 3: The Works of Shakespeare

Each round has two phases:

  • Phase 1: You have 5 minutes to write down everything your team can recall about the round's topic — from memory or your existing notes only. No new research.
  • Phase 2: Take turns sharing one fact. Cross off facts given by other teams.
    • You earn 1 point per unique fact given!
    • A valid fact must be true and specific — vague statements like "Shakespeare was famous" do not count. A fact like "Shakespeare was born in Stratford-upon-Avon in 1564" does.
    • Stealing: A team may steal a point by providing a specific detail the sharing team left out — but only if they had both the original fact and the additional detail already written on their paper. See detailed rules & examples 📜

⚖️ Stealing Rules

When can a team steal?
A team may attempt to steal a point when the sharing team states a fact but omits an important specific detail. To make a valid steal, the challenging team must meet both of the following conditions:

  1. They must have the original fact written on their paper.
  2. They must have the additional specific detail also written on their paper — it cannot be something they thought of on the spot after hearing the other team speak.

If both conditions are met, the challenging team states their more complete version. The teacher has final say on whether the added detail is specific and significant enough to earn the steal. A steal that is awarded transfers the point entirely — the original team does not keep it.

Examples

✓ Valid Steal

Team A says: “Shakespeare was born in 1564.”
Team B challenges: “We have written: Shakespeare was born on April 23, 1564, in Stratford-upon-Avon, England.”
Result: Team B had both the birth-year fact and the specific date and location already on their paper. The steal is valid — the point goes to Team B.

✗ Invalid Steal — Detail Not Written Down

Team A says: “Shakespeare was born in Stratford-upon-Avon in 1564.”
Team B challenges: “We know the exact date was April 23rd!”
Result: Team B can only show “1564” on their paper — the specific date was not written down before Team A spoke. The steal is not valid. The point stays with Team A.

✗ Invalid Steal — Detail Not Specific Enough

Team A says: “Shakespeare wrote comedies, tragedies, and histories.”
Team B challenges: “He also wrote sonnets!”
Result: Sonnets are a genuinely new fact, not a missing detail of Team A’s statement. Team B should save that for their own turn. No steal.

Remember: the teacher always has final say. When in doubt, be generous to the defending team.
